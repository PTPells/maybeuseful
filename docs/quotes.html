Steen Eiler Rasmussen: Experiencing Architecture

We get to the point where we cannot describe our
impression of an object without treating it as a living
thing with its own physiognomy. For even the most precise
description, enumerating all visible characteristics, will
not give an inkling what we feel is the essence of the
thing itself. Just as we do not notice the individual

it is that we perceive but only of the conception created
