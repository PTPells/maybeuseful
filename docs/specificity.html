Being specific

History

I heard Lin Manuel Miranda dismiss the tension
people describe between making something specific
its potential for universal appeal.[i][ii]

Since hearing Miranda name specificity as a property
common among impactful work, I started noticing
other people making a similar observation.

This observation describes my experinece
and I’m curious why this is so. Why
might specificity continue to show up in
work that affects people? What does work
that is specific feel like? Why have I felt
challenged by being specific? Why does specificity
tend to be cast as being in tension with appeal?
Why is specificity even something that feels notable
enough to talk about?

I’m motivated to sketch answers to these
questions so that I can hold myself
accountable to being specific in what I
make and say. I also want to develop the
awareness to notice when I'm not being specific
and become curious about why this might be so.

In short, I think specificity is common among
impactful ideas beacuse to be specific there must
be something real and inside of that reality is potential.

The potential for people “receiving” the work
to see and feel the reality its “creators” have
experienced and are attempting to communicate through
it. And with a clear message embedded legibly within
the work, people can do all sorts of things.

People can:

  • See the idea
  • Visit the reality the idea lives within
  • Relate the idea to their experience
  • Sense how the idea makes them feel
  • Imagine themselves integrating the
    idea into their life
  • Form an opinion for if/how integrating
    the idea into their life would impact them
  • Describe the idea using language that
    feels intuitive to them
  • Recognize the opportunities to share the
    idea with others
  • Combine the idea with something they
    have thought about
  • Remember the idea
  • Decide whether they will allow the idea to
    change them
  • Evolve how they see and understand the reality
    they experience

It’s notable to me how each of the scenarios listed above
is open-ended. They do not suggest what happens next.
While specificity creates the potential for people
to understand, experience, and talk about an idea,
a specific idea does not have the ability
to control the ways in which people react and handle it.
By definition, people can reonsate with and build on an
idea that is specific as easily as they can criticize
it, dismiss it as small, and declare it "wrong."

I think it's the fear of experienceing the second half of
the sentence above that can sometimes scare me away of being
specific.
