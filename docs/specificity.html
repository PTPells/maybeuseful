Being specific

People have named specificity as a
property common among impactful ideas.

This observation describes my experinece
and I’m curious why this is so. Why
might specificity continue to show up in
work that impacts people? What does work
that is specific feel like? Why do poeple
tend to cast specificity as being in
tension with impact? Why is specificity
even notable enough to talk about?

I’m motivated to sketch answers to these
questions so that I can hold myself
accountable to being specific in what I
make. I also want to develop the awareness
to notice when I'm not being specific and
become curious about why this might be so.

In short, I think specificity is common among
impactful ideas beacuse specificity creates
potential.

The potential for people “receiving” the work
to see and feel the message its “creators” are
attempting to communicate through it. And with
a clear message embedded legibly within the work,
people can do all sorts of things.

People can:

  • Relate the idea to their experience
  • Sense how the idea makes them feel
  • Imagine themselves integrating the
    idea into their life
  • Form an opinion for if/how integrating
    the idea into their life would impact them
  • Describe the idea using language that
    feels intuitive to them
  • Recognize the opportunities to share the
    idea with others
  • Combine the idea with something they
    have thought about
  • Remember the idea
  • Integrate the idea into their everyday life

It’s notable to me how each of the scenarios listed above
is open-ended. They do not suggsest what happens next.
Said another way: while specificity creates the potential
for people to understand, evaluate, and talk
about an idea, a specific idea does not have the ability
to control the ways in which people react and handle it.
By definition, people can celebrete and relate to an
idea that is specific as easily as they can criticize
it and dismiss it as small.

When I couple the uncertainty of how people might
react with how inertia can cause me to blur the
distinction between my self and the idea I have a part
in, two things become clear to me:

  1. Being specific begets intimacy and vulnerability
  2. Being generic produces distance and elusiveness

Back to the questions I started with:

  • Why do poeple tend to cast specificity
    as being in tension with impact?
  • Why is specificity even notable
    enough to talk about?

LOOSE
I wonder if this has to do with the culture in which specificity is being discussed. Perhaps, the culture that is developing ideas does not have memory of haivng created something from scratch that's been impactful. and with that experience they lack the conviction to "stand" in specificity. I also wonder whether when people say an idea needs to be bigger, what they're actually talking about is the ambition of the idea, and the impact htey hope it to have rather than about the idea itself.
